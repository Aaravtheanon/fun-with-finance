Investing Guide

Explore the basics of investing with easy, step-by-step instructions and helpful links!

Start Learning

1) Mutual Funds

Step 1: Understand the Basics

A mutual fund pools money from many investors to invest in stocks, bonds, or other assets. It's managed by professional fund managers. Great for beginners!

AMFI (India) SEC Investor (US)

Step 2: Choose a Fund

Research different AMC (Asset Management Companies). Compare performance, fees (expense ratio), and objectives to align with your goals.

Morningstar

2) SIP (Systematic Investment Plan)

Step 1: Set a Monthly Amount

SIP allows you to invest a fixed amount regularly (e.g., monthly) into a mutual fund or other instrument. Decide how much you can invest comfortably.

Step 2: Automate It

Link your bank account and set up an auto-debit. This helps maintain consistency and build wealth over time.

SIP Examples

3) Pension Yojna

Step 1: Pick a Government Scheme

If you’re in India, consider Atal Pension Yojana or the National Pension System (NPS). Compare coverage, monthly contributions, and eventual returns.

NPS Official Site Atal Pension Yojana

Step 2: Registration & KYC

You can apply online or visit your bank. Provide KYC (ID/address proof). Decide your monthly or annual contributions based on your goals.

4) PPF (Public Provident Fund)

Step 1: Eligibility & Documents

In India, most residents are eligible. Have ID, address proof, and other KYC docs ready. PPF has a 15-year lock-in but offers tax benefits.

Official PPF Info

Step 2: Open the Account

Open a PPF at designated banks or post offices. Some allow online applications. Deposit a minimum each year to keep the account active.
